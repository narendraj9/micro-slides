RISC vs CISC

8051: CISC
ARM and PIC: RISC

CISC

  • Optimized for low code density.
  • Provides more high level instructions.

RISC

  • Optimized for low power.
  • Simpler and smaller CPU designs.

Here's a good read: RISC vs CISC

Machine Models

PIC and 8051 : A mix of Accumulator-Register & Register-Memory
ARM: Register-Register

Machine Models

  • ARM family of architectures is 32-bit.
  • 8051 and PIC have 8-bit long data-buses.
Endianness
  • ARM processors are bi-endian. Instructions to change the endianness. SETEND
  • PIC and 8051 are little-endian processors.

Links: Endianness and ARM Big and Little Endian

Pipeline
  • ARM processors support a large number of pipeling stages
    • ARM7 and earlier designs had 3-stage pipelines: Fetch, decode and execute. ARM Cortex-A8 has 13 stages in the pipeline.
  • 8051 and PIC are very primitive in this case. The next instruction is fetched while the previous one is executing.
